# HR-Analytics-Dashboard

Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using the IBM HR Analytics dataset. The objective is to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable business recommendations for improving employee retention.

The dashboard was built entirely in Microsoft Excel using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, KPI Cards, Slicers, and interactive dashboard design techniques.

Compared attrition among employees who worked overtime versus those who did not.

Key Insights
Research & Development department recorded the highest attrition (133 employees).
Laboratory Technicians showed the highest turnover among all job roles (62 employees).
Young employees experienced the highest attrition (91 employees).
Low-income employees represented the largest attrition group (113 employees).
Employees working overtime accounted for 54% of total attrition.
Young and low-income employees were identified as the highest-risk employee segment.
Business Recommendations
Reduce excessive overtime workload.
Improve retention programs for Laboratory Technicians.
Review compensation policies for lower-income employees.
Strengthen engagement and career development initiatives for younger employees.
Implement targeted retention strategies for high-risk employee groups.
Project Outcome

The dashboard provides HR teams and management with a clear view of employee attrition patterns, enabling data-driven decisions to improve workforce retention and employee satisfaction.
